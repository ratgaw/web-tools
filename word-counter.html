How the counts are calculated

Words are counted by splitting on whitespace, so hyphenated words and contractions count as one word each. Sentences are counted by looking for text ending in ., !, or ? — it's a simple heuristic, so abbreviations with periods can occasionally throw the count off by one or two. Paragraphs are counted as blocks of text separated by a blank line. Read time is estimated at 200 words per minute, a common average adult silent-reading speed.

Common uses: staying under a Twitter/X character limit, hitting a minimum word count for an assignment, checking a meta description fits Google's ~155-character display limit, or estimating how long a blog post or script will take to read aloud.
