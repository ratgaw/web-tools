How these color harmonies work

Each mode picks hues using a real relationship on the color wheel rather than pure randomness: complementary pairs opposite hues for maximum contrast (good for a call-to-action button against a background), analogous picks neighboring hues for a calm, cohesive feel, triadic spaces three hues evenly for vibrant variety without clashing, and monochrome varies only the lightness of a single hue for a minimal, tightly controlled look. Random skips the theory entirely for quick, unstructured inspiration.

Every swatch shows its hex code, which you can click to copy directly — paste it straight into CSS, a design tool, or a style guide. Regenerating gives you a new palette in the same harmony, so you can keep rolling until something clicks.
